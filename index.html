💉 Vaccination Guide

  • Routine Immunization: Following the complete IAP schedule is crucial for your child's immunity.
  • Painless vs Painful: Painless (Acellular) vaccines cause significantly less fever, pain, and swelling than whole-cell vaccines.
  • Post-Vaccine Care: Do not massage the injection site. Use a cool cloth for swelling.
  • Missed Dose? Don't panic. Consult us to reschedule safely. Catch-up doses are highly effective.
×

🥦 Detailed Nutrition Guide

  • Infants (0-1 Year): Exclusive breastfeeding for the first 6 months. Introduce iron-rich purees and mashed fruits at 6 months. Avoid salt, sugar, and cow's milk entirely before age 1.
  • Toddlers (1-3 Years): Transition to family foods. Ensure 3 main meals and 2 healthy snacks. Incorporate full-fat dairy for brain development, lean proteins, and colorful vegetables.
  • Essential Micronutrients:
    • Iron: Dates, jaggery, spinach, and lean meats (crucial for IQ and preventing anemia).
    • Calcium & Vitamin D: Milk, yogurt, ragi, and morning sunlight for strong bones.
    • Healthy Fats: Ghee, butter, nuts powder, and avocados for nervous system growth.
  • Hydration: Ensure adequate water intake after 6 months. Avoid packed juices and sodas.
  • Picky Eaters: Do not force-feed. Offer a variety of textures and colors. Keep meal times screen-free and pleasant.
×

📱 Screen Time & Eyes

  • 0-2 Years: Zero screen time. Brain development relies on real-world interaction.
  • 2-5 Years: Max 1 hour/day of educational content.
  • 20-20-20 Rule: Every 20 mins, look 20 feet away for 20 seconds.
×

🤱 Exclusive Breastfeeding

  • First 6 Months: Strictly breast milk. Breast milk is 88% water, so NO extra water needed.
  • On Demand: Feed whenever the baby shows hunger cues.
  • Benefits: Higher IQ, superior immunity against infections.
×

🥣 Complementary Feeding

  • When to Start: Exactly after 6 months of age.
  • First Foods: Start with single-grain cereal, thick dal water, or mashed apple.
  • Texture: Start smooth, move to lumpy mashed foods by 8 months.
