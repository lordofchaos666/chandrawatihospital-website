Growth & Pregnancy Trackers

Month-by-month milestones and clinical guidance for parents and mothers-to-be.

Baby Growth Tracker
Select your baby's age to review expected milestones and care points.

1 Month

Growth

Regains birth weight. Gains 20–30g per day on average.

Milestones

Briefly follows objects with eyes. Startles to loud noises (Moro reflex present).

Feeding

Exclusive breastfeeding. 8–12 feeds per day. No water or formula unless medically indicated.

2 Months

Growth

Steady weight gain. Noticeable increase in skin-fold thickness.

Milestones

Social smile develops. Holds head up briefly in prone position.

Clinical Note

6-week vaccinations due. Continue exclusive breast milk. Head circumference monitoring important.

4 Months

Growth

Birth weight usually doubles by 4–5 months.

Milestones

Rolls over (prone to supine). Babbles. Reaches for and grasps objects.

Feeding

Exclusive breast or formula milk. Weaning not yet recommended.

6 Months

Growth

First tooth may erupt (lower central incisor). Height increases significantly.

Milestones

Sits without support. Responds to own name. Stranger awareness begins.

Feeding

Initiate complementary feeding. Introduce smooth single-ingredient purees. Continue breastfeeding.

9 Months

Milestones

Crawling. Pulls to stand. Pincer grasp developing.

Social / Language

Stranger anxiety peaks. Understands "No". Babbles with intonation.

Feeding

Lumpy mashed foods. Finger foods introduced. 3 meals + 2 snacks daily.

12 Months

Growth

Birth weight has tripled. Length increased by ~50%.

Milestones

First independent steps. Says "Mama" / "Dada" with intent. Waves bye-bye.

Feeding

Family foods in soft texture. Cow's milk can be introduced. Breastfeeding may continue.

Pregnancy Tracker
Track fetal development and maternal changes trimester by trimester.

Month 2 (Weeks 5–8)

Maternal Changes

Morning sickness, fatigue, and urinary frequency. Breast tenderness common.

Foetal Development

Neural tube closes. Cardiac activity detectable by USG (~6 weeks).

Clinical Note

Initiate Folic Acid (5mg/day). Book antenatal USG. HbA1c and blood group testing advised.

Month 4 (Weeks 13–16)

Maternal Changes

Second trimester begins. Energy levels return. Nausea typically resolves.

Foetal Development

Digits, facial features, and external genitalia well-formed. Foetal movements begin.

Clinical Note

Begin left-lateral position during sleep. Anomaly scan (Level II USG) scheduled at 18–20 weeks.

Month 6 (Weeks 21–24)

Maternal Changes

Visible gravid uterus. Fetal movements clearly perceived. Varicosities may appear.

Foetal Development

Eyes open partially. Responds to auditory stimuli. Viability threshold approaching.

Clinical Note

GCT (Glucose Challenge Test) for GDM screening. Iron and calcium supplementation review.

Month 8 (Weeks 29–32)

Maternal Changes

Dyspnoea as fundal height compresses diaphragm. Oedema of ankles common. Backache increases.

Foetal Development

Rapid cerebral growth. Lung surfactant production underway. Fat deposition increases.

Clinical Note

Growth scan. Finalize birth plan. Prepare hospital bag. Review delivery mode discussion.

Month 9 (Weeks 33–40)

Maternal Changes

Braxton-Hicks contractions. Lightening (descent of presenting part). Cervical changes begin.

Foetal Development

Term foetus. Fully mature organ systems. Head engaged in primigravidae.

Clinical Note

Report immediately: regular strong contractions, rupture of membranes, absent fetal movements, or bleeding.

Vaccination Guide

  • Routine Immunization ScheduleFollowing the complete IAP schedule ensures your child is protected against 11+ vaccine-preventable diseases.
  • Painless vs. Whole-Cell VaccinesAcellular (DTaP) vaccines cause significantly less post-vaccination fever, injection-site swelling, and distress than whole-cell (DTwP) vaccines. Both provide equivalent immunity.
  • Post-Vaccination CareDo not massage the injection site. Apply a cool cloth for local swelling. Low-grade fever (≤38.5°C) for 24 hours is expected — paracetamol may be used as advised.
  • Missed Dose ProtocolDo not restart the series. Catch-up schedules are available for all IAP vaccines. Consult us to reschedule without delay.

Paediatric Nutrition Guide

  • Infants (0–6 Months)Exclusive breastfeeding. Breast milk is 88% water — no supplemental water required. Formula is acceptable when breastfeeding is not possible.
  • Toddlers (1–3 Years)Three main meals with two healthy snacks. Full-fat dairy for brain myelination. Lean proteins, colourful vegetables, and complex carbohydrates.
  • Essential Micronutrients
    • Iron: Dates, jaggery, spinach, and lean meats — critical for cognitive development and prevention of iron-deficiency anaemia.
    • Calcium & Vitamin D: Milk, yogurt, ragi, and morning sunlight for bone mineralisation.
    • Healthy Fats: Ghee, nuts powder, and avocado — essential for CNS growth and myelination.
  • HydrationAdequate water after 6 months. Avoid packaged juices, aerated drinks, and added sugars before age 2.
  • Picky EatersAvoid force-feeding. Offer variety in texture and colour. Maintain screen-free, distraction-free meal times.

Screen Time & Eye Health

  • 0–2 YearsZero screen time. Brain development at this stage depends on real-world interaction, language input, and tactile stimulation.
  • 2–5 YearsMaximum 1 hour per day of high-quality, supervised educational content.
  • 20-20-20 RuleEvery 20 minutes, look at an object 20 feet away for 20 seconds to reduce digital eye strain.
  • Bedtime ScreensNo screens for at least 1 hour before sleep. Blue light suppresses melatonin and disrupts circadian rhythm in children.

Exclusive Breastfeeding

  • First 6 MonthsStrictly exclusive breast milk. Breast milk contains 88% water — no supplemental water is required even in hot climates.
  • Feeding on DemandFeed whenever the infant shows hunger cues: rooting, lip-smacking, hand-to-mouth movements. Crying is a late hunger cue.
  • Evidence-Based BenefitsExclusive breastfeeding is associated with 15+ point higher IQ scores, superior mucosal immunity, reduced risk of type 1 DM, obesity, and allergic disease.
  • Latch and PositioningCorrect latch is crucial. Consult our lactation counselor if pain, engorgement, or insufficient milk supply is suspected.

Complementary Feeding

  • TimingInitiate complementary feeding at exactly 6 completed months. Not before (risk of aspiration, allergy) and not after (iron deficiency, growth faltering).
  • First FoodsSingle-ingredient foods: thick dal water, mashed banana, apple puree, or rice porridge. Introduce one new food every 3–5 days.
  • Texture ProgressionSmooth purees (6 months) → lumpy mashed (8 months) → soft chopped finger foods (10 months) → family foods (12 months).
  • Foods to AvoidNo salt, sugar, honey (botulism risk), cow's milk as main drink, or whole nuts before age 1.
